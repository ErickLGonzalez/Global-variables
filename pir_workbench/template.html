Honesty key — how to read a fact (SOUND vs HEURISTIC · evidence E0–E4 · L-level · warnings)
SOUND analyzer output rests on exact/interval-certified reasoning. HEURISTIC ⚠ is not certified — a HEURISTIC or E3 result must never be read as a proof.
Evidence level (orthogonal to L): E0 exact theorem/arithmetic · E1 interval-certified · E2 statistical w/ stated coverage · E3 simulation-conditioned · E4 proxy/indirect.
PIR level (structural altitude): L0 raw records · L1 operational acts · L2 structural facts · L3 candidate grammars.
Verdict is SPEC-locked: FORCED, PERMITTED, REJECTED, NONIDENTIFIABLE, OBSERVATIONALLY_EQUIVALENT, APPARATUS_LIMITED, REPRESENTATION_DEPENDENT, AMBIGUOUS. Facts with no verdict carry raw structure only.
⚠ a fact carries located warnings — hover or expand the row to read them verbatim.

Provenance & assumption invalidation

Select an assumption; the workbench replays FactStore.invalidate_assumption offline (direct dependants + transitive closure over depends_on_facts) and highlights every fact that would be DOWNGRADED. Nothing committed is mutated.

Verdict × evidence matrix

Counts of facts by SPEC verdict (rows) and evidence level (columns). Scarcity of high-evidence certified verdicts is meant to be visible at a glance — colour tracks evidence, not desirability.

Candidate lattice

GVAR rules fire on the shared predicate set and emit candidate grammar families plus their discriminating test obligations. The lattice returns a SPEC-locked verdict; equivalent families are retained in parallel as L3 hypotheses, never pruned.
